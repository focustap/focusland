# Rooftop Runner Polish Log

## Preservation Targets

- Keep the identity of a fast rooftop runner built around flow, combo, and stylish recovery.
- Preserve upper-route risk/reward, grappling, wall interactions, vaulting, and speed-building through skill.
- Keep the game scoped to a compact arcade loop rather than expanding into a larger progression game.

## Current Weak Points

- Movement has good ideas, but punishment is too binary in places and the slide is not central enough.
- Obstacles are mechanically varied but visually abstract, which hurts readability at speed.
- The eight areas exist in code, but they do not yet feel like eight memorable districts with distinct mood and obstacle emphasis.
- The player reads as prototype stick art instead of a clickable arcade character.
- HUD and menu flow are functional but not convincing as a finished arcade game.
- Rewards run a little hot for weak runs and the early seconds need more cinematic clarity.

## Planned Passes

1. Stabilize movement feel, recovery windows, and slide utility.
2. Rework obstacle readability, fairness, and pattern pacing.
3. Increase procedural variety and give each district stronger identity.
4. Upgrade art direction, background depth, sprites, and moment-to-moment effects.
5. Polish title, HUD, pause, game over, and summary presentation.
6. Rebalance rewards and long-run difficulty.
7. Run repeated build and screenshot review passes, then sand down remaining rough edges.
